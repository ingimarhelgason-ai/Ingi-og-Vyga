Vi har bygget ting med vores hænder i lang tid. Vi er stolte af vores arbejde, vi møder til tiden, og vi går ikke, før det er rigtigt.

We know that renovation projects can be stressful. We don't just show up as carpenters — we show up as your partners. And when obstacles come up, because they always do, we find a solution and keep things moving. We are respectful of your home, we understand that things change, and we are flexible when they do.

Við vitum að endurbótaverkefni geta verið streituvaldandi. Við mætum ekki bara sem trésmíðar — við mætum sem samstarfsaðilar þínir. Mes žinome, kad renovacijos projektai gali būti įtemptus. Mes neatvykstame tiesiog kaip staliai — mes atvykstame kaip jūsų partneriai. Restaurant · Full Renovation · 2023

Dirty Burger
& Ribs —
Fellsmúli

The Project

This was previously an Aktu-Taktu restaurant, so the kitchen layout was already in place. The brief was clear — give it a completely new identity inside and out.

Outside, we stripped the old cladding and installed our own — acid-washed metal sheets treated to look like they had been burned. The result is a bold, dark exterior that immediately tells you this is no ordinary burger joint. We were very happy with how it came out, and felt we really delivered the unique look the Dirty Burger team was after.

Inside, we stripped everything back to bare walls. We laid new vinyl flooring throughout the dining area — chosen for its durability and water resistance. We built two completely new bathrooms, one for customers and one for staff, with full tiling in both and around the service counter area.

The signature piece is the service counter — clad in the same burned steel plates we used outside, tying the whole identity together. Parts of the dining room walls were finished the same way. We worked closely with an interior designer who created the vision, and we brought in a plumber and electrician to handle those trades. Everything else was us.

Residential · Exterior Cladding · 2018

Cladding —
Kópavogur

The Project

Full exterior cladding on a residential house in Kópavogur — dark corrugated aluminium installed in winter 2018. The frame goes up first, then insulation is fitted between, and finally the cladding goes on — every panel tight, level and square.

Dark smábára is one of the most popular choices in Reykjavík right now, and it's easy to see why. It looks sharp, it's completely weatherproof, and once it's on it needs nothing from you. Ever.

It's also one of the most cost-effective cladding options available. The corrugated sheets are only 0.8mm thick compared to flat metal panels which are typically 2mm — that's a significant material saving without any compromise on durability or appearance. You get a premium result at a fraction of the price of other metal solutions.

In many ways smábára is the classic Icelandic exterior — as much a part of the Reykjavík streetscape as colourful plaster houses are to Rome. We think it looks great, and this project is a good example of why.
