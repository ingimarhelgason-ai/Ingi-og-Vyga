Restaurant · Full Renovation · 2023

Dirty Burger
& Ribs —
Fellsmúli

The Project

This was previously an Aktu-Taktu restaurant, so the kitchen layout was already in place. The brief was clear — give it a completely new identity inside and out.

Outside, we stripped the old cladding and installed our own — acid-washed metal sheets treated to look like they had been burned. The result is a bold, dark exterior that immediately tells you this is no ordinary burger joint. We were very happy with how it came out, and felt we really delivered the unique look the Dirty Burger team was after.

Inside, we stripped everything back to bare walls. We laid new vinyl flooring throughout the dining area — chosen for its durability and water resistance. We built two completely new bathrooms, one for customers and one for staff, with full tiling in both and around the service counter area.

The signature piece is the service counter — clad in the same burned steel plates we used outside, tying the whole identity together. Parts of the dining room walls were finished the same way. We worked closely with an interior designer who created the vision, and we brought in a plumber and electrician to handle those trades. Everything else was us.

Residential · Exterior Renovation · 2023

Cladding,
New Windows
& Glass Railing —

The Project

The brief on this project was to take a two-storey residential house and make it completely weatherproof — with zero maintenance required for decades to come. We covered the entire exterior in aluminium corrugated cladding, known in Icelandic as smábára, and laid new electrical wiring for outdoor lighting throughout.

The cladding work isn't just about aesthetics — it's a practical decision that protects the structure from Iceland's harsh weather for the long term. Once it's on, it's on. No painting, no patching, no upkeep.

After the cladding was complete, we installed the glass railing on the balcony. During that process, the decision was made to extend the glass railing to the exterior stairs as well — something that required careful planning but came out extremely well. The thinking behind it was simple: the space had felt tight and enclosed before, and glass lets the light through. It keeps everything feeling open and spacious without sacrificing safety.

Residential · Full Interior Renovation · 2022

Full Interior
Fit-out —
Úlfarsárdalur

The Project

This house had previously been converted into two separate rental apartments. Our job was to join them back together into a single home and bring the whole property up to the highest possible standard — essentially starting from scratch.

The first step was stripping the house back to the bone. We removed the apartment division and dismantled everything that needed replacing — walls, ceilings, floors, plumbing routes, electrical runs. Once we had a clean canvas, the real work began.

Almost all the electrical wiring was replaced and reconfigured from scratch. Floor heating was installed throughout, and new stairs were built to connect the floors properly as a single home again. We framed and boarded new walls and ceilings, plastered, filled, and painted every surface.

We worked alongside an interior designer throughout to make sure the finishing details matched the vision. The result is a modern, high-quality home that shows no trace of the two-apartment layout it came from.
